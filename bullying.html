Some words DO hurt more than others

Elementary school staff often underestimate the number of students involved in frequent bullying. While the majority of the school staff estimated that less than 10% of their students were bullied in the past month, over 30% of the students actually said they were frequently bullied

Safe students are more successful students

Students who report being harassed are twice as likely to have their personal belongings stolen or deliberately damaged at school; are more likely to skip school and have lower grades and are up to 4 times as likely to have been threatened with a weapon at school

Young students see and know more than we think

When students report a more severe bullying climate, school-wide passing rates on standardized tests are up to 5% lower

Facts

In the US, 1 in 5 students ages 12-18 has been bullied during the school year

Students who reported that they were frequently bullied scored lower in reading, mathematics, and science than their peers who reported that they were never or rarely bullied

Approximately 160,000 teens have skipped school because of bullying
